CORE TALKING POINTS
Point 1 Start with Sadhguru as a person
›

1. START WITH SADHGURU AS A PERSON

You can begin by introducing Sadhguru as a contemporary yogi and mystic, known for bringing powerful inner tools in a very practical, down-to-earth way.

Example you could say
“Instead of speaking only from scriptures or philosophy, Sadhguru always brings it down to how you can handle your mind, emotions and body right now — whether you’re a student, a parent, or a CEO.”
  • He is the founder of Isha Foundation, a non-profit, volunteer-run organisation.
Example
“Isha Foundation is almost entirely volunteer-driven. Even big events and programs are run by ordinary people like us who have benefited from the tools and simply want to share them.”
  • He offers tools for inner wellbeing rather than asking people to believe anything.
Example
“Sadhguru doesn’t ask you to believe in him or any ideology. He gives you simple practices — like specific yogic tools and ways to sit, breathe, and look at life — and says, ‘Just experiment and see what it does to you.’”
  • His work spans spirituality, education, ecology, and rural upliftment.
Example
“For example, apart from spiritual programs, there are schools for rural children, large tree-planting and river revitalisation efforts, and many projects to support villages and farmers. It’s a very hands-on approach to uplifting human life.”
Point 2 Explain what Isha Foundation is
›

2. EXPLAIN WHAT ISHA FOUNDATION IS

Isha is a global, volunteer-based organisation dedicated to human wellbeing. It offers classical yoga programs and runs many social and environmental projects.

Example
“You’ll find Isha centres in India and around the world. Everywhere it’s the same spirit — volunteers offering classical yoga and different initiatives to support people’s physical, mental, and inner wellbeing.”
  • Centres and ashrams where people can learn powerful yogic practices.
Example
“At the Isha Yoga Center, people can do programs ranging from simple weekend workshops to immersive retreats where you learn practices that you can continue at home every day.”
  • Programs like Inner Engineering that help people handle stress, anxiety, and the pressures of modern life.
Example
“For many of us, Inner Engineering was the first step. It helped us handle stress, sleep better, and respond more calmly at work and at home — without changing our jobs or families, just changing how we experience things inside.”
  • Initiatives such as education projects and large-scale ecology movements.
Example
“Isha is also behind education projects for rural children and big ecological movements like large-scale tree planting and soil revitalisation efforts, so the impact is both inner and outer.”
Point 3 Keep it relatable
›

3. KEEP IT RELATABLE

For most people, the best entry point is how this has touched your own life. After you share who Sadhguru and Isha are, bring it back to your experience.

Example framing
“After hearing all this, what usually connects most is a simple sharing: ‘This is how it changed me.’ People remember your experience more than a long list of activities.”

What changed for you after Inner Engineering or other programs?

Example
“Before Inner Engineering, I used to get overwhelmed very easily — small things at work would stay in my head the whole night. After doing the program and practicing regularly, I noticed I could go through the same situations but stay much more stable inside.”

How has your day-to-day life, relationships, or work been affected?

Example
“In my day-to-day life, I don’t react as quickly as before. With family, I find it easier to listen instead of arguing. At work, my focus and efficiency improved because my mind is not constantly scattered.”

One or two simple examples are often more powerful than long explanations.

Example
“You could say something as simple as: ‘Earlier I used to wake up already feeling tired and anxious. Now, after doing my practices, I start the day with more clarity and energy.’ Short, real stories like that touch people.”

Volunteer tip: Let the other person speak. Ask what they are looking for in their own life — more balance, clarity, joy, health — and share how these tools supported you in that direction.

Example question
“You can gently ask, ‘In your own life right now, what would you like more of — calmness, energy, focus, better health?’ Then you can add, ‘For me, these practices helped exactly in that area, that’s why I continue to do them.’”

SUGGESTED 3–5 MINUTE FLOW

30–45 seconds: Who is Sadhguru (person, not a concept).

Example
“Sadhguru is a contemporary yogi and mystic who has created powerful inner tools to help people handle their mind, emotions, and body. He’s the founder of Isha Foundation, which is a volunteer-run non-profit.”

60–90 seconds: What is Isha and what it offers.

Example
“Isha offers classical yoga programs like Inner Engineering, which help people deal with stress and find clarity, and also runs many projects in education, ecology, and rural upliftment. It’s a global volunteer-based organisation dedicated to human wellbeing.”

60–90 seconds: Your personal experience & key change.

Example
“After doing Inner Engineering, I noticed my anger reduced, my sleep improved, and I became more joyful without any external change. That’s why I felt it was worth volunteering so others can experience this too.”

30–60 seconds: One simple next step they can take.

Example
“If any of this resonates with you, the simplest next step is to try Inner Engineering or even start with a short program/online session. I can share the official page where you can explore upcoming programs.”

Tip: You don’t have to say it exactly like this. Use the timing as a guide and adapt the language to your own way of speaking.

COMMON QUESTIONS TO BE READY FOR

“Is this a religion or do I have to believe something?”

Example response
“You don’t have to believe anything. These are tools for inner wellbeing, like a technology for your body and mind. You simply practice and see what happens within you — no belief or conversion is required.”

“Is it safe to do these programs?”

Example response
“The programs are designed and refined over many years, and millions of people across different countries and backgrounds have done them. If someone has a specific medical condition, they are guided to follow the health guidelines and, if needed, check with their doctor.”

“How much time or commitment does it need?”

Example response
“Most foundational programs are structured so that even people with busy schedules can do them. After that, the daily practices usually take a limited amount of time each day, like any other health routine — but you can experience benefit even from that small investment of time.”

You do not need to answer everything in depth. It is enough to share what you know clearly, and direct them to official Isha pages or volunteers when needed.

Example
“If you’re not sure, you can simply say: ‘I don’t know the full technical explanation, but I can share the official link or connect you with someone who can guide you further.’ That’s perfectly fine.”

Anchor line you can use:
“I am not here to convince you of anything. I have simply seen that these tools have helped me. If you feel it could be useful for you, I can share how to explore it.”

How you might feel using this
This line keeps the conversation open and respectful. You’re simply sharing what has worked for you and leaving the choice completely with them.
