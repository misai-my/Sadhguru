Isha Volunteer Intro Kit

01 · Introduce Sadhguru & Isha

This page helps you give a simple, grounded introduction to who Sadhguru is, what Isha Foundation does, and why Inner Engineering and other offerings matter for people in your life.

Audience: Friends, family & colleagues hearing about Sadhguru/Isha for the first time.

Core talking points
1. Start with Sadhguru as a person

You can begin by introducing Sadhguru as a contemporary yogi and mystic, known for bringing powerful inner tools in a very practical, down-to-earth way.

  • He is the founder of Isha Foundation, a non-profit, volunteer-run organisation.
  • He offers tools for inner wellbeing rather than asking people to believe anything.
  • His work spans spirituality, education, ecology, and rural upliftment.
2. Explain what Isha Foundation is

Isha is a global, volunteer-based organisation dedicated to human wellbeing. It offers classical yoga programs and runs many social and environmental projects.

  • Centres and ashrams where people can learn powerful yogic practices.
  • Programs like Inner Engineering that help people handle stress, anxiety, and the pressures of modern life.
  • Initiatives such as education projects and large-scale ecology movements.
3. Keep it relatable

For most people, the best entry point is how this has touched your own life. After you share who Sadhguru and Isha are, bring it back to your experience.

  • What changed for you after Inner Engineering or other programs?
  • How has your day-to-day life, relationships, or work been affected?
  • One or two simple examples are often more powerful than long explanations.
Volunteer tip: Let the other person speak. Ask what they are looking for in their own life — more balance, clarity, joy, health — and share how these tools supported you in that direction.
How to use this page
Suggested 3–5 minute flow
  1. 30–45 seconds: Who is Sadhguru (person, not a concept).
  2. 60–90 seconds: What is Isha and what it offers.
  3. 60–90 seconds: Your personal experience & key change.
  4. 30–60 seconds: One simple next step they can take.
Common questions to be ready for
  • “Is this a religion or do I have to believe something?”
  • “Is it safe to do these programs?”
  • “How much time or commitment does it need?”

You do not need to answer everything in depth. It is enough to share what you know clearly, and direct them to official Isha pages or volunteers when needed.

Anchor line you can use:
“I am not here to convince you of anything. I have simply seen that these tools have helped me. If you feel it could be useful for you, I can share how to explore it.”
